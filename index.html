Knowledge Distillation Cross Domain Diffusion Models for Weakly Supervised Defect Detection in Semiconductor Manufacturing

Yuan Fu Yang
Min Sun
[Paper]
[GitHub]

Abstract

Detecting defects during the semiconductor manufacturing process is crucial for improving productivity and yield. This paper presents a novel weakly supervised approach called the Implicit Cross-Domain Diffusion Model (ICDDM) for defect detection. ICCDM is designed to train a generative model to infer the joint distribution of images over defect patterns and reference wafer maps (background circuit) as a Markov chain by minimizing a denoising score matching objective. In addition, we employ a diffusion process of ICDDM in the low dimensional latent space to enhance the efficiency of the inference process and introduce a knowledge distillation method that approximates a multi-step generative process by evaluating a single step function. By training a student model to learn the denoising process in latent space, we significantly reduce the number of denoising iterations while keep the model performance, leading to faster inference times.

Implicit Cross-Domain Diffusion Model for Semiconductor Defect Dection

We introduce a deterministic iterative noising and denoising scheme that facilitates image-to-image translation between defect and defect-free wafers. During the training stage, we employ a dual-domain Markov chain for image-to-image translation task, which approximates the data distribution of the defect pattern domain and circuit map domain such that they are correlated with each other. Then, ICDDM learns to progressively denoised to the circuit map from defect pattern. During the inference stage, we utilize a reversed sampling scheme of ICDDM to generate a detailed representation of a circuit map image and obtaining a pixel-wise defect pattern by subtracting the original image from a synthetic image generated during the denoising process. Through this procedure, our method effectively preserves the fine details of the input image that are not affected by the defect while re-painting the defect part with realistic looking circuit map.
